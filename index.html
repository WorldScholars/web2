Meet The Professor

Course program

This course provides a comprehensive exploration of the history and principles behind AI-generated music. In the initial week, we trace the evolution of computer-generated music, covering milestones like the MIDI revolution and algorithmic composition, while also introducing the basics of digital audio. Subsequent weeks delve into signal-level representation, synthesis techniques, and symbolic-level compositions using tools like Markov Chains and Cellular Automata. The course progresses to the application of neural networks for both symbolic and signal-level automated composition, utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs) for music creation. The final segment focuses on the intersection of computer science and music through machine learning-driven recommendation systems. Assignments include reflective writing, creating sound synthesis procedures, modifying provided code for music generation, and exploring recommendation system concepts.

Introduction to AI-generated Music

Syllabus

1. History of Computer Generated Music and Basics of Digital Audio

Week 1

This lecture explores the evolution of computer-generated music from its early beginnings to the present day, covering key milestones such as the MIDI revolution, synthesizers, algorithmic composition. We will also cover digital audio basics - introducing both the signal and symbolic representations of music.

Synthesis Techniques

Week 2

We look into different ways that sound is represented on the computer, and how to manipulate this to understand the meaning of sound. We cover the differences between what we hear and what is stored on the computer. We also explore basic techniques for the construction of sound.

Automated composition with Markov Chains and Cellular Automata.

Week 3

We look a human-understandable representations of audio on the computer and how to construct such representations. We cover two basic techniques from the “first wave” of AI composition - Markov Chains and Cellular Automata.

Automated Composition with Neural Networks

Week 4

We use neural networks to compose music based on a dataset of existing pieces. We discuss various techniques for working on both the symbolic level and the signal level of audio. We also explore applications of LLMs to create code that generates music.

Recommendation Systems

Week 5

We cover one of the core industrial applications of computer science to music, which is recommendation systems. We cover some basics of the concept and explore various approaches to recommendation systems for music.
